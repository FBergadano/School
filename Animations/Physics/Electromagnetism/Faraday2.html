Faraday's Second Experiment

A conducting coil is connected to a light bulb. Moving a magnet in or out of the coil induces a current — and lights the bulb. The faster the motion, the brighter the light.
Drag the magnet to try it.

Key observation

A stationary magnet — however strong — induces no current. Only a changing flux matters: moving in, moving out, or changing the number of turns all affect the current. Reversing the magnet reverses the current direction.
